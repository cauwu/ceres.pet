APPENDIX 1

Responses from people when asked about the webcams, showing them pictures

—

An exploration into building digital electronics in a way that changes how people think about them. By suspending webcams in conductive wire, the idea was to make people less apprehensive about using webcams—if you can see the physical line where the power to the camera sensor is coming from, you know that if the switch in the line isn’t closed, there’s no way it can be on.

An intriguing form and features are important to make people concentrate on the little details that would make the concept effective, dramatic lines drawn with wire help draw people in.

On asking people about how they thought of these webcams I found out that they didn’t help them feel less apprehensive about using webcams, but that they did feel intrigued by them in a way that made them want to learn more about them.

—

Asked them questions:

- do you think it would make you less anxious around webcams if you could physically see the wiring inside of it, the sensor, and the gap in the wiring when it's switched off, for example, in an object something like what I've made

- does seeing electronics presented in this way make you want to learn about how they work, does it intrigue you to see a webcam all blown up in wire like this

- do you feel like you understand the webcam better seeing it like this

The questions are obviously leading and not unbiased, but I wanted the responses to be conversational, as I was asking people I already knew and explained them my project.

—

To get my results from this question, I again looked at how people spoke about each prototype. I read their tone, level of excitement or disinterest, and recorded statements or points that I considered to be important toward my goal of making objects that create intrigue.

People liked the 1st and 3rd one generally.

Of the 2nd one, people said that it was simple and conveyed the wiring well, but the 3rd one makes them want to follow the wiring more. Another said that it was less exciting than the 3rd one

Some people liked the character of the 3rd, they said it was like a pet, friend, alien. One said that the 3rd one makes them want to fiddle with it.

Of the 1st one, one person said that it’s the only one they would recognise as something they could take apart, but that the 3rd one was better as an art piece. Another person said the mousetrap vibes of the 1st one made them very curious

Summary: It seems like people are most interested in the features of 1 and 3. that probably means no sheets over the wiring, and a wire frame that meshes with the electrical traces.
