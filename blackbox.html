BLACK BOX

A speculative project about rapidly prototypeable, handmade digital electronic things and the cultures people create around them. 2023

Craft practice is a social process. It requires a craft practitioner to be constantly learning from others, constantly exhibiting and looking for feedback, and constantly exploring their surroundings and themselves for what makes their practice unique.

Craft practitioners form communities of teaching, learning and exhibition. Those communities forge friendships and love, life-defining skills and hobbies, and social groups that support each other.

Black Box is a speculative project about a craft community forming around handmade digital electronics.

It's hard to learn about digital electronics. This project began as a way to explore that learning process and find out what what can motivate people to learn and make for themselves, in a hobby setting.

I undertook it in a nonscientific way. I darted around concepts, introducing things through prototyping and testing. I didn't start with an analysis of the conditions of learning digital electronics as a hobby, I relied on my own prior experiences and the experiences of people that I knew. It's not a scientific project, and I never set out to prove a point, but rather to see where the prototyping and asking questions took me, hoping to end up with a design project by the end of it.
